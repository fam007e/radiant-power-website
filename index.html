Radiant batteries

A betavoltaic battery generates electric power from radioactive decay, converting the kinetic energy of beta particles into electrical energy. It consists of a semiconductor material, like silicon or diamond, forming a p-n junction with specific impurities. When beta particles from a radioactive material, such as tritium, strike the semiconductor, they create electron-hole pairs, generating a voltage difference. Betavoltaic batteries have a long lifespan and compact size but low power densities.
